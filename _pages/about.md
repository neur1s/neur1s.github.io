    I'm a PhD candidate in Neuroscience at University of Toronto (Physiology program). I like exploring memory in biological and artificial systems. My primary PhD project is supervised by Dr. Sheena Josselyn and focuses on the role of the amygdala in social memory. I study this in mice using neurosurgeries, optical recordings of neural activity, pharmacological interventions, and behavioral assays. In collaboration with Dr. Brokoslaw Laschowski, I recently developed a brain-inspired framework for memory prioritization in neural networks.
